📌 Level 02 · Accounting · L2.03 — Cash Flow Statement

The Cash Flow Statement is the most honest of the three financial statements. Unlike the income statement, it cannot easily be distorted by accrual accounting choices. It shows how much cash actually entered and left the business.

Three Sections, One Purpose

SectionWhat it capturesKey items
Operating (CFO)Cash from the core business — independent of financingNet income + D&A ± working capital changes
Investing (CFI)Cash used to invest in or divest assetsCapex, acquisitions, disposal proceeds
Financing (CFF)Cash flows related to capital structureDebt raised/repaid, dividends, share buybacks
Net Change in Cash = CFO + CFI + CFF Opening Cash + Net Change = Closing Cash (ties to Balance Sheet cash line)

Free Cash Flow — the metric that matters most

FCF = CFO minus Capex. It is the cash generated after maintaining and investing in the asset base. It is the numerator in DCF valuation, the metric that determines debt serviceability, and the ultimate measure of cash-generating quality.

Why CFO beats Net Income

Net income can be managed through accruals. Cash is binary. Rising net income with falling CFO is a red flag — investigate working capital and capitalisation policies. A company with low net income but strong CFO (high D&A in an asset-heavy business) may be far more cash-generative than its P&L suggests.

Common Mistake

Treating negative CFI as always bad. A company spending heavily on capex or acquisitions will show large negative CFI — but if these investments earn above WACC, they are value-creative. Analyse the purpose and return on CFI, not just the sign.

In a Real Bank

When building an LBO model, the debt repayment schedule is driven entirely by FCF projections. An analyst who gets the CFO section wrong — mismodelling working capital or D&A — will produce an incorrect debt paydown schedule and a materially wrong IRR. The CFS section of a model is where junior analysts make the most errors.

🔑 Lender's Perspective — JaceyTrayd

We require quarterly CFS submissions from all borrowers. Our primary monitoring metric: FCF / Debt Service (interest + scheduled principal). Below 1.2x triggers a covenant review. Below 1.0x means the borrower cannot service debt from operations — an escalation to credit committee and a potential default notice.
