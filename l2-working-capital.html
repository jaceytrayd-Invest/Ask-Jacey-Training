📌 Level 02 · Accounting · L2.05 — Working Capital

Working capital is the cash tied up in day-to-day operations. It is invisible on the income statement but has an enormous impact on cash flow, deal value, and debt serviceability. Understanding it is essential before you touch a model.

Definition and Components

Net Working Capital = Current Operating Assets - Current Operating Liabilities + Accounts Receivable (money owed to you) + Inventory (stock held) - Accounts Payable (money you owe suppliers) - Accrued Liabilities (expenses incurred, not yet paid) - Deferred Revenue (cash received before revenue earned)

Cash Impact of WC Changes

What happensCash impactWhy
AR increases (customers pay slower)Use of cash (negative)Revenue recognised; cash not yet received
Inventory increasesUse of cash (negative)Cash spent to build stock before sale
AP increases (pay suppliers slower)Source of cash (positive)Supplier extending you interest-free credit
AR decreasesSource of cash (positive)Cash collected; receivable eliminated

Working Capital in Deal Context

In M&A, working capital is negotiated as part of deal terms. A "normalised working capital peg" is the WC level required to run the business at closing. Completion accounts adjust the price for any deviation — WC shortfall reduces price, surplus increases it.

Modelling WC

Model each WC line as days of the relevant P&L line: DSO (AR/Revenue x 365), DIO (Inventory/COGS x 365), DPO (AP/COGS x 365). Track the 5-year trend and apply consistent assumptions in the forecast. Changes in the WC cycle affect FCF every year — small assumption changes compound significantly over a 5-year model.

🔑 Lender's Perspective — JaceyTrayd

We monitor DSO and DPO quarterly. Rising DSO compresses FCF and signals collection risk. We sometimes take a pledge over the debtor book — a priority claim over receivables in default. This is the basis of asset-based lending, where the borrowing base is sized against eligible receivables and inventory, marked down by advance rates reflecting their realisable value.
