Featured Lesson — The Pitch Book

📍 Deal Execution · The Pitch Book · L9.01

The pitch book is the IB analyst's primary deliverable and the document that wins mandates. Building one well — accurately, coherently, under time pressure — is the core craft of junior banking.

Anatomy of a Sell-Side M&A Pitch Book

SectionPurposeAnalyst role
1. Situation OverviewOrient the client; demonstrate deep understanding of their business and current contextDraft company overview; pull financial metrics; format cover
2. Strategic ContextEstablish why a transaction makes strategic sense nowResearch sector M&A activity; write industry narrative
3. Valuation — Football FieldAnchor price expectations with a defensible, multi-methodology rangeRun trading comps, precedents, DCF; build the football field chart
4. Buyer UniverseShow the breadth and quality of likely acquirer interestResearch each buyer; estimate rationale and capacity; rank
5. Process RecommendationAdvise on how to structure the sale — broad vs. targeted, timeline, exclusivity approachDraft process design section; prepare indicative milestone timeline
6. CredentialsProve the bank can execute what it is proposingPull relevant tombstones; format team CVs; update deal log

The Football Field — the most important slide

Construction: For each methodology, calculate LOW and HIGH implied equity value range: Trading Comps: 25th–75th percentile peer multiples Precedent Txns: 25th–75th percentile deal multiples DCF: Sensitivity range (WACC ±1%, terminal growth ±0.5%) 52-week range: Actual observed market price range (listed targets) Analyst targets: Range of published analyst price targets (if listed) Plot as horizontal bars on a single chart Add reference lines: current share price, proposed offer price Label each bar with the implied EV or equity value range The zone where all bars overlap = most defensible value range. The offer price should sit within or above this overlap zone to withstand a board fairness challenge.

Pitch Book Craft Principles

One idea per slide. If a slide takes more than 15 seconds to understand, it has too much. The MD presenting cannot explain your slide and watch the client simultaneously. Cut ruthlessly.

Titles are conclusions, not labels. "Revenue growth has accelerated to 18% CAGR" beats "Revenue Growth 2021–2024." The reader should not need to read the chart to understand the point.

Consistency signals professionalism. Font sizes, decimal places, number formats, alignment — every inconsistency is a signal. Clients subconsciously assess whether this bank can manage a complex process. Sloppy formatting is an early negative signal they often cannot articulate but will feel.

The 48-Hour Pitch Crunch

Most pitch books are built in 48–72 hours. The MD gets the call Monday; the pitch is Thursday. The analyst's job in those 72 hours: research the company and sector, build the model, run all three valuations, research the buyer universe, format every slide, and incorporate five rounds of senior review — each arriving at 11pm. The ability to produce accurate, professional work under this timeline is the single most important skill assessed in the first 12 months of an analyst's career.

In a Real Bank

Banks maintain proprietary pitch book templates — standardised slide designs, fonts, and colour schemes enforced across all client materials. The template ensures brand consistency. Goldman's books look like Goldman's. That visual identity, built over decades, is part of the trust the client places in the institution. Deviation requires MD approval. This is not bureaucracy — it is brand management.
