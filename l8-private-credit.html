📌 Level 08 · Private Markets · L8.04 — Private Credit — Direct Lending

Private credit has emerged as one of the fastest-growing areas in finance — a $1.7 trillion asset class that now regularly displaces banks and syndicated markets for mid-market corporate lending. Direct lenders like JaceyTrayd provide capital faster, more flexibly, and with more bespoke terms than traditional lenders. This is the operating model explained from the inside.

The Private Credit Advantage in M&A Financing

For a PE sponsor completing a £150m mid-market buyout, private credit offers three things a bank syndicate cannot: (1) Speed — JaceyTrayd can provide a credit-approved term sheet in 2–3 weeks; a bank syndicate takes 6–8 weeks with execution risk. (2) Certainty — one lender who has committed capital; no syndication risk if markets move. (3) Flexibility — PIK toggle if the company needs to conserve cash in Year 1; deferred amortisation; bespoke covenant package tailored to the specific business. The sponsor pays 100–150bps more in margin — but these three benefits are worth that cost on almost every mid-market deal.

In a Real Bank — Leveraged Finance Perspective

The rise of private credit has been the single biggest structural shift in leveraged finance over the past decade. Banks that previously arranged and held leveraged loans now find themselves increasingly excluded from the mid-market segment (sub-£500m). The IB leveraged finance response has been to: (1) raise their own credit funds (Goldman's GSAM, Morgan Stanley's IM, Barclays Credit Funds), competing directly with independent direct lenders; (2) focus on larger transactions where syndication efficiency justifies the complexity; (3) provide the analytics and origination that direct lenders sometimes lack, earning arrangement and advisory fees on deals they then refer to private credit for execution.
