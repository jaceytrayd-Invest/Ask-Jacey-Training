📌 Level 05 · M&A · L5.04 — Synergies — Modelling & Reality

Synergies are the incremental value created by combining two businesses that neither could generate alone. They are the primary justification for paying a control premium. They are also the most frequently overstated and underdelivered element of any M&A deal.

Categories of Synergies

CategoryExamplesRealisation timelineCertaintyTypical valuation multiple
Cost synergiesHeadcount (duplicate functions), facilities consolidation, procurement savings, IT rationalisation12–24 monthsHigh — plannable and executable10–12× after-tax run-rate
Revenue synergiesCross-selling, geographic expansion, combined pricing power, new product capabilities24–48 monthsLow — depends on customer behaviour and market conditions6–8× after-tax run-rate
Financial synergiesHigher debt capacity (combined entity can carry more leverage), interest tax shield, tax structure optimisationImmediate to 12 monthsMedium — modellable but tax advice requiredCapitalise the tax shield via APV or include in WACC

How to Model Synergies

Step 1: Identify synergies bottom-up Headcount: List roles by function; identify overlapping teams Facilities: Map all locations; identify closures Procurement: Combined spend × estimated saving % Revenue: Customer overlap analysis × cross-sell conversion rate Step 2: Apply realisation schedule Year 1: typically 30-50% of run-rate (while integration ongoing) Year 2: 70-85% of run-rate Year 3: 100% run-rate (fully phased in) Step 3: Calculate one-time integration costs Redundancy payments, lease break costs, IT migration, advisory fees Typically 1.0-2.0× the annual run-rate synergy as a one-time cost Step 4: Net present value of synergies PV(synergies) = PV of phased synergy cash flows (discounted at WACC) Net synergy value = PV(synergies) − PV(integration costs)

Synergy Reality Check

The empirical record on synergy delivery is poor. Studies consistently find:

Synergy type% of stated synergies actually achieved (studies average)Average delay vs. plan
Cost synergies~70–80%3–6 months
Revenue synergies~30–40%12–24 months
Total synergies~50–60%6–18 months
The Synergy Haircut

Conservative buyers apply a "synergy haircut" — modelling only 50–70% of identified synergies at a 20–30% longer timeline than management claims. This is not pessimism — it is statistical accuracy. A buyer who models full synergy realisation on schedule is taking on the risk that their bid is too high if synergies come in at 50%. Haircut the synergies; set the price based on the haircut; let any upside be captured in returns, not priced in at entry.

🔑 Lender's Perspective — JaceyTrayd

We model zero synergies in our base case credit analysis. If the deal only works (i.e., the business can service the debt) because of synergies, we will not lend — we cannot take synergy execution risk. In our upside case, we model 50% of the stated cost synergies (never revenue synergies in a lending context). The required EBITDA headroom must exist in the business as it is today, without any help from the combination. This is a hard rule that has protected us from every synergy overpromise we have seen in practice.
