📌 Level 04 · Modelling · L4.03 — The 3-Statement Model

The 3-statement model is the skeleton of every financial analysis in IB. Master it completely. Every error in a more complex model (LBO, DCF, merger model) can be traced back to a broken linkage in the underlying three statements.

The Build Sequence

Always build in this order. Out-of-sequence building creates dependencies that are impossible to resolve cleanly.

1. Historical data input (3+ years of actuals) 2. Income Statement forecast (formulas from assumptions tab) 3. Debt Schedule (calculates interest; creates circularity) 4. Cash Flow Statement (starts with Net Income from IS) 5. Balance Sheet (rolls forward using IS, CFS, and debt schedule) 6. Balance check (Assets = Liabilities + Equity, every year)

Income Statement Build

Revenue ← assumptions tab (growth rate × prior year) − COGS ← assumptions tab (1 − gross margin %) = Gross Profit − SG&A ← assumptions tab (% of revenue or fixed growth) − R&D ← assumptions tab (% of revenue) = EBITDA − Depreciation & Amortisation ← from PP&E roll-forward or assumptions = EBIT (Operating Profit) − Interest Expense ← from debt schedule + Interest Income ← cash balance × interest rate on cash = EBT (Pre-tax Profit) − Income Tax ← EBT × effective tax rate = Net Income

Cash Flow Statement Build — Indirect Method

OPERATING ACTIVITIES: Net Income ← direct link from IS + Depreciation & Amortisation ← same D&A as IS (non-cash add-back) − Increase in AR ← -(AR_end - AR_start) [if AR↑, cash↓] − Increase in Inventory ← -(Inv_end - Inv_start) + Increase in AP ← (AP_end - AP_start) [if AP↑, cash↑] + Increase in Accrued Liabs ← (AccL_end - AccL_start) = Cash Flow from Operations (CFO) INVESTING ACTIVITIES: − Capital Expenditure ← assumptions tab (% of revenue or fixed) ± Acquisitions / Disposals ← deal-specific = Cash Flow from Investing (CFI) FINANCING ACTIVITIES: ± Debt raised / repaid ← from debt schedule − Dividends paid ← assumptions tab (payout ratio × NI) ± Equity issued / bought back ← deal-specific = Cash Flow from Financing (CFF) NET CHANGE IN CASH = CFO + CFI + CFF CLOSING CASH = Opening Cash + Net Change

Balance Sheet Roll-Forwards

ASSETS: Cash = Prior cash + Net change in cash (CFS bottom line) AR = Revenue × DSO / 365 Inventory = COGS × DIO / 365 PP&E (net) = Prior PP&E + Capex − D&A Goodwill = Prior goodwill − impairments (or fixed) Other assets = prior year or formula-driven LIABILITIES: AP = COGS × DPO / 365 Accrued liabs = Revenue or OpEx × accrual days / 365 Short-term debt = Current portion from debt schedule Long-term debt = Debt schedule closing balances EQUITY: Share capital = Prior + new equity issuance Retained earnings = Prior RE + Net Income − Dividends Other reserves = Prior + other comprehensive income BALANCE CHECK: Assets − (Liabilities + Equity) = 0 (must be zero)
The Retained Earnings Link — The Most Missed Connection

Retained Earnings is where the income statement permanently joins the balance sheet. RE (end) = RE (start) + Net Income − Dividends. Every year's net income adds to equity through this mechanism. If your RE roll-forward is wrong, every equity figure in the model is wrong, the balance sheet won't balance, and your equity-level valuation metrics are incorrect. Double-check this link is formula-driven and references net income directly from the IS.

In a Real Bank

Building a standalone 3-statement model for a new company from scratch (pulling and inputting historical data, setting up all linkages, building the debt schedule, writing the formulas) typically takes a junior analyst 4–8 hours for a company they know well, and up to 2 days for a complex corporate with multiple segments and non-standard balance sheet items. Speed comes with repetition — most experienced analysts can build a clean model in 3–4 hours. Practice until this is automatic.
