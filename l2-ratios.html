📌 Level 02 · Accounting · L2.07 — Key Ratios

Accounting ratios translate raw financial data into comparative, actionable metrics. A single ratio in isolation is almost meaningless — insight comes from comparing to peers, to the company's own historical trend, and to industry benchmarks.

Profitability Ratios

RatioFormulaIB Use
Gross MarginGross Profit / RevenuePricing power. Declining gross margin = cost or competitive pressure.
EBITDA MarginEBITDA / RevenuePrimary valuation input. Used in EV/EBITDA comps and LBO modelling.
EBIT MarginEBIT / RevenueCapital-structure-neutral operating profit measure.
Net MarginNet Income / RevenueAfter-tax bottom line. Affected by leverage; use EV multiples for cross-company comparison.
ROCEEBIT / Capital EmployedEfficiency of total capital. If ROCE > WACC, the company creates value.
ROENet Income / Shareholders EquityReturn to equity holders. Can be inflated by leverage (DuPont: ROE = Net Margin x Asset Turnover x Leverage).

Leverage & Credit Ratios

RatioFormulaLender significance
Net LeverageNet Debt / EBITDAPrimary covenant metric. IG: below 3x; HY: 3-5x; LBO entry: 5-7x.
Interest CoverEBITDA / Interest ExpenseDebt serviceability. Lenders require above 2.0-2.5x minimum.
FCF / Debt ServiceFCF / (Interest + Principal)True coverage including amortisation. Must exceed 1.0x or borrower cannot service debt.

Efficiency (Working Capital) Ratios

RatioFormulaWhat to watch
DSO(AR / Revenue) x 365Rising DSO = slower cash collection. Watch for AR growing faster than revenue.
DPO(AP / COGS) x 365Rising DPO = slower supplier payment. Can signal liquidity stress or strong supplier leverage.
DIO(Inventory / COGS) x 365Rising DIO = inventory building. Possible demand slowdown or production inefficiency.
Cash Conversion CycleDSO + DIO - DPOLower CCC = faster cash conversion. Negative = cash-generative WC model.
Context is Everything

A 40% EBITDA margin is mediocre for software, excellent for industrials, and impossible for distribution. A 6x net leverage ratio is fine for a stable utility, catastrophic for a cyclical manufacturer. Always benchmark against sector peers and the company's own 5-year history — never interpret a single ratio in isolation.

🔑 Lender's Perspective — JaceyTrayd

Our loan documentation typically includes 3-4 financial covenants: leverage (Net Debt/EBITDA stepping down from 6.0x to 4.5x over the loan term), interest cover (EBITDA/Interest above 2.5x), capex cap, and minimum liquidity. Tested quarterly. A covenant breach triggers renegotiation rights — not an automatic default — but gives us significant leverage to improve terms or require early repayment.
