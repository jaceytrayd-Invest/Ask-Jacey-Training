📌 Level 11 · Advanced · L11.01 — Restructuring & Distressed Situations

Restructuring is what happens when a company's obligations exceed its ability to pay them. It is the most adversarial, intellectually demanding corner of IB — combining valuation, legal mechanics, negotiation strategy, and human judgment under conditions of maximum pressure. Every banker who works on leveraged transactions needs to understand this.

The Distress Spectrum

StageMeaningOptions
Liquidity stressShort-term cash shortage; company is fundamentally solvent (assets > liabilities) but cannot meet near-term obligationsRCF draw, emergency bridge, asset sales, equity cure
Covenant breachFinancial covenants breached — technical default but no cash default yet. Lender has acceleration rights but not exercisingWaiver and reset, margin step-up, equity cure, A&E
Balance sheet insolvencyLiabilities exceed assets at fair market value. Debt trading significantly below par.Out-of-court restructuring, debt-for-equity swap, scheme of arrangement
Cash flow insolvencyCannot pay obligations as they fall due — imminent payment defaultAdministration, Chapter 11, pre-pack, liquidation

The Priority Waterfall

In any restructuring or insolvency, distributable value flows in strict order: 1. Administration / restructuring costs and professional fees 2. DIP financing (new money provided during the process — super senior) 3. Super-senior RCF (if contractually super-senior) 4. Senior Secured First Lien (TLB) 5. Senior Secured Second Lien 6. Senior Unsecured (HY bonds, trade creditors) 7. Subordinated / Mezzanine debt 8. Preferred equity 9. Common equity ← typically wiped out The FULCRUM SECURITY is the class where EV runs out. Above fulcrum: recover at or near par At fulcrum: partial recovery; these holders become the new equity owners Below fulcrum: recover little or nothing Identifying the fulcrum early determines who has negotiating leverage.

The JaceyTrayd Approach to Distressed Credits

When a portfolio credit enters stress, our objectives are precisely ordered: first, protect par recovery; second, preserve the ongoing relationship; third, maximise recovery beyond par if possible. Our loan documents are designed to give early warning (maintenance covenants), adequate security (first-ranking charge), and equity cure mechanisms. When enforcement becomes necessary, we move quickly — extended uncertainty is more damaging to recovery value than decisive action.

In a Real Bank

Restructuring advisory is a specialised, counter-cyclical IB product. Dedicated restructuring groups at Houlihan Lokey, Lazard, Rothschild, Moelis, and within bulge brackets advise either the distressed company (debtor-side) or the creditor group (creditor-side). The work is counter-cyclical — busier in recessions when most IB revenue falls. Restructuring bankers are among the most technically sophisticated in the industry, combining deep financial modelling with legal mechanics and negotiation strategy simultaneously.
