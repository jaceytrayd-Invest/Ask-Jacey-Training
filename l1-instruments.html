📌 Level 01 — Foundations · L1.02 — Financial Instruments — Equity, Debt & Derivatives

Every transaction in IB involves one or more financial instruments. Before you can value them, structure them, or advise on them, you need to understand what each instrument is, how it is priced, and what claim it gives its holder.

Derivatives

Derivatives are instruments whose value derives from an underlying asset. They are used for hedging (reducing risk) and speculation (taking risk).

InstrumentWhat it isIB use
Options (calls/puts)Right (not obligation) to buy/sell at a strike priceEmployee stock options; M&A deal protection; convertible bonds
Futures/ForwardsObligation to buy/sell at a fixed price on a future dateFX hedging for cross-border deals; commodity price exposure
Interest Rate SwapsExchange fixed for floating interest payments (or vice versa)Converting floating-rate loans to fixed for budgeting certainty
CDS (Credit Default Swap)Insurance against a borrower defaulting on their debtHedge fund credit trading; bank balance sheet management
In IB — When Each Instrument Appears

In an M&A deal: the target's shares (equity) are acquired; the acquirer may issue new shares (equity) or raise debt (bonds or loans) to fund the purchase. In an LBO: the PE firm uses a mix of equity and debt (leveraged loans + high yield bonds) to acquire the target. In an IPO: the company creates and sells new common stock in the primary market. Knowing which instrument is used and why is the context for every transaction you will work on.
