📌 Level 00 — Orientation · L0.05 — IB Divisions — Where Do You Fit?

Investment banking is not one job — it is a collection of distinct businesses operating under one roof. Knowing the divisions, how they interact, and which suits your skills and ambitions is essential before you start applying.

Product Groups vs. Coverage Groups

IBD is structured along two axes: product groups (what they do) and coverage groups (who they serve). Most analysts rotate between both in their first 2 years before specialising.

TypeFocusKey role
Product groupsExecute specific transaction types across all sectorsTechnical specialists — deep expertise in one product (M&A, LevFin, ECM)
Coverage groupsCover all products for companies in a specific sectorRelationship managers — know the clients, originate deals, coordinate with product groups

The Coverage Groups

Coverage bankers are sector specialists. They know the companies in their sector, their management teams, and their strategic priorities. They originate deal mandates by maintaining relationships year-round. When a deal is mandated, product groups execute it.

Common coverage groups: TMT (Technology, Media, Telecoms) · Healthcare · Consumer & Retail · Industrials · Energy & Natural Resources · FIG (Financial Institutions) · Real Estate · Infrastructure

Which is right for you?

If your goal is buy-side PE: target M&A or Leveraged Finance. These groups build the modelling depth and deal experience PE firms recruit for.

If your goal is hedge funds or equity investing: ECM gives you public market exposure and relationships with institutional investors.

If your goal is staying in IB long-term: coverage groups offer more client relationship work and are the route to MD. Product MDs exist but coverage MDs have more origination upside.

If your goal is distressed / turnaround: Restructuring is the most intellectually demanding group and produces the best distressed PE alumni.

In a Real Bank

In a live deal, product and coverage groups work together on every transaction. The coverage banker (who knows the client) runs point; the M&A or LevFin team does the execution. There is constant internal competition for credit — who "owns" the client relationship, who gets the fee credit. As an analyst, your primary allegiance is to your group — but you learn to navigate the matrix by making yourself indispensable to as many teams as possible.
