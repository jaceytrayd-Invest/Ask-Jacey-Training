📌 Level 08 · Private Markets · L8.01 — Private Equity — Structure & Economics

Private equity is one of the most consequential forces in modern finance — buying companies, transforming them, and selling them at a profit. Understanding the fund structure, GP/LP economics, the carried interest waterfall, and the competitive dynamics of the industry is the foundation for everything in this level.

The PE Fund Structure

Limited Partnership structure: General Partner (GP) = The PE Firm - Contributes 1-3% of fund capital (skin in the game) - Makes all investment decisions - Manages portfolio companies - Earns: management fee + carried interest - Unlimited liability (in theory) Limited Partners (LPs) = The Investors - Contribute 97-99% of fund capital - Receive returns net of fees and carry - No role in investment decisions - Limited liability (cannot lose more than committed capital) - Types: pension funds, endowments, sovereign wealth funds, insurance companies, family offices, fund-of-funds Fund vehicle: typically a Cayman Islands or Delaware LP Management company: the GP's operating entity (fee recipient) Investment period: typically 5 years (deploy capital) Fund life: typically 10 years (deploy + realise = 5+5) Extensions: 1-2 years possible with LP consent

The Carried Interest Waterfall

Standard American waterfall (deal-by-deal): Step 1: Return of capital LPs receive 100% of distributions until full capital returned Step 2: Preferred return (hurdle rate, typically 8% IRR) LPs receive 100% of distributions until 8% annual return achieved Step 3: GP catch-up GP receives 80-100% of distributions until GP has earned 20% of all profits distributed to date Step 4: Carried interest Profits split 80% LP / 20% GP indefinitely European waterfall (fund-level): GP only earns carry after ALL investments are realised and LPs have received full capital + preferred return across the entire fund — more LP-protective; standard in European PE
Why Carry Is the Most Powerful Incentive in Finance

A 20% carry on a $5 billion fund that returns 2.5x gross (generating $7.5bn profit above cost) = $1.5bn carry pool. Typically shared among 10-30 senior professionals over a 7-10 year period. A senior partner at a top PE fund can earn $20-50m per year in carry — multiples of any comparable salary-based compensation. This is why talented professionals from investment banking, consulting, and operating roles target PE as a career destination: the economics are structurally superior for those who generate top-quartile returns.

In a Real Bank — Coverage Perspective

PE sponsors are among the most important clients of leveraged finance and M&A advisory at every major investment bank. A sponsor relationship — particularly with a large fund (Blackstone, KKR, Apollo, Carlyle, CVC) — generates repeat business across: buy-side advisory on acquisitions, sell-side advisory on exits, debt financing on every deal, refinancing during the hold period, and potential IPO mandates on exit. A single PE sponsor relationship can be worth £50-200m of advisory and financing fees to a major bank over a 10-year period.
