📌 Level 11 · Advanced · L11.05 — Special Situations — Hostile M&A & Spin-offs

Special situations are M&A transactions outside the standard sell-side auction. Hostile bids, contested situations, and corporate separations require different analytical frameworks, different legal considerations, and a different relationship dynamic between the bank and its client. This is where senior banker judgment matters most.

Hostile Takeovers — attack and defence

PhaseAttacker's actionDefender's response
Pre-announcementQuietly accumulate shares below disclosure threshold (3% UK, 5% US); engage advisors; build the strategic rationaleMonitor unusual share price activity; ensure shareholder register is monitored; maintain IR relationships
Private approachConfidential approach to target CEO/Chairman — both courtesy and legal requirement in some jurisdictions; rejection expectedBoard considers and formally rejects; engages defence advisors; activates defence playbook
Public announcementAnnounce offer directly to shareholders at 20–40% premium; simultaneously publish strategic rationale and synergy caseBoard issues "reject" recommendation; begins searching for white knight; may activate defensive measures
Bid periodRoadshow to institutional shareholders; publish synergy analysis; engage proxy advisors (ISS, Glass Lewis); potentially raise the offerPublish counter-arguments; question synergy credibility; seek white knight; if UK: Takeover Panel constrains defences severely
ResolutionAchieve 50%+ acceptance (UK) or majority control (US); or withdraw if support insufficientBoard recommendation becomes decisive if white knight found; otherwise shareholders decide

UK vs. US — the regulatory difference

UK TAKEOVER CODE (Takeover Panel): → Board cannot implement poison pill without shareholder approval → Board cannot sell assets or issue new shares to frustrate a bid → Once a Rule 2.7 announcement is made, strict timetable applies → Offer must be unconditional within 60 days of posting → Shareholders decide — board can recommend but not block → Result: hostile bids are more viable in UK US (Delaware / state law): → Boards can adopt rights plans (poison pills) defensively → Staggered boards slow the bidder's ability to replace directors → Shareholder approval required for many defensive measures but the board has significantly more flexibility than in UK → Result: hostile bids are harder in US; more defences available

Why Spin-offs Typically Create Value

Academic evidence consistently shows that spin-offs generate positive abnormal returns for parent shareholders in the 1–2 years following separation. The mechanism: removing the conglomerate discount by allowing specialist investors to value each business on its own terms. The spun-off subsidiary typically re-rates to a premium multiple once it trades independently; the parent simplifies and re-rates as a focused business. The announcement of a spin-off is typically the most value-creating corporate event a conglomerate can announce short of a full sale.

In a Real Bank

Spin-off advisory is one of the highest-fee structural advisory mandates. A major spin-off (e.g., GlaxoSmithKline spinning off Haleon in 2022) requires: separation accounting (carving 3 years of standalone financials from group accounts), governance structuring (independent board, audit committee, executive team), debt allocation (negotiating who takes what debt), transition service agreements, two separate listing processes, and dual regulatory submissions. The advisory fee reflects this complexity: typically 0.25–0.50% of the separated entity's enterprise value, which on a £5bn spin-off equals £12.5–25m.
