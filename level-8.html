Featured Lesson — Private Credit: Direct Lending

📍 Private Markets · Private Credit · L8.04 · JaceyTrayd

Private credit has grown from a niche corner of finance into a $1.7 trillion asset class that now rivals the syndicated loan market for mid-market transactions. Understanding how direct lenders think, underwrite, and structure is essential for any IB professional working on leveraged transactions — and is the core operating model of JaceyTrayd.

The Unitranche — private credit's flagship product

The unitranche is a single blended facility combining first-lien and second-lien characteristics into one instrument at one blended rate. It replaces the traditional syndicated loan stack (TLB + HY bonds + RCF) with a single lender relationship.

Traditional syndicated LBO stack (£400m deal): TLB (£240m): SOFR + 425bps 2nd Lien (£80m): SOFR + 775bps HY Notes (£40m): Fixed 9.5% RCF (£40m): SOFR + 375bps (undrawn) Weighted average cost: ~8.5% | Timeline: 6-8 weeks | 15+ lenders Unitranche equivalent (£360m): Single facility at SOFR + 600bps RCF: SOFR + 450bps (same lender) All-in cost: ~11.5% (higher) | Timeline: 3-4 weeks | 1-2 lenders The borrower pays MORE for the unitranche — in exchange for: - Speed and certainty of execution - Single relationship (one lender to negotiate with) - Confidentiality - Covenant flexibility - No syndication risk For PE sponsors on mid-market deals, this trade-off is almost always worth it.

The JaceyTrayd Credit Process

Every credit begins with the same discipline: understand the downside before quantifying the upside. Our five-stage underwriting process is designed to answer one question with increasing certainty: in a stressed scenario, can we get our money back?

The JaceyTrayd Credit Philosophy

We lend against FCF, not EBITDA. EBITDA is an accounting construct — FCF is what actually services debt. Our primary covenant is EBITDA/Interest, but our internal underwriting is FCF/Total Debt Service (including amortisation). If FCF does not cover total debt service in the downside scenario, we do not lend — regardless of how healthy EBITDA looks.

We never lend on synergies. Management synergies are aspirational. We underwrite the business as it exists today — not as it might be after a transformation plan that requires 24 months and significant execution risk. We model zero synergies as our base case and haircut management projections by 15–25% before beginning our credit analysis.

The Private Credit Mistakes That Create Losses

Lending on pro forma EBITDA without verification. A management-adjusted EBITDA of £50m that includes £15m of "synergies not yet achieved" is really £35m. Lenders who accept pro forma figures without independent quality-of-earnings verification have overstated the borrower's debt capacity by 43%.

Under-pricing the complexity premium. A unitranche for a complex, covenant-heavy business requires significantly more ongoing monitoring than a plain-vanilla credit. If the spread does not reflect the monitoring burden and the illiquidity of the position, the risk-adjusted return is below target even if the credit performs.

In a Real Bank — Leveraged Finance Perspective

The leveraged finance team at a bank is the sell-side equivalent of the private credit function. They structure and arrange leveraged loans and HY bonds for PE-backed companies, then syndicate them to institutional investors (including direct lenders like JaceyTrayd). The IB leveraged finance analyst must understand the credit from both perspectives simultaneously: the borrower's ability to service debt (the lender's lens) and the attractiveness of the yield to investors who will buy the paper (the capital markets lens). Bridging these two perspectives is the core technical skill of leveraged finance.
