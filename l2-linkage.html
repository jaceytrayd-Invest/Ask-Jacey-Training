📌 Level 02 · Accounting · L2.04 — Three-Statement Linkage

The three statements are not independent documents — they are a single system. Every economic event touches all three, and the linkages between them are the foundation of every financial model you will ever build.

The Five Mechanical Links

Link 1 — Net Income flows to the Cash Flow Statement (starting line)

The CFS indirect method begins with net income. It then adds back non-cash charges (D&A) and adjusts for working capital changes to arrive at CFO.

Link 2 — Net Income flows to Retained Earnings on the Balance Sheet

Net income less dividends flows into Retained Earnings in the equity section. This is how profit accumulates as equity over time.

Retained Earnings (end) = Retained Earnings (start) + Net Income - Dividends

Link 3 — Net change in cash ties the CFS to the Balance Sheet

The bottom of the CFS is the net change in cash. This equals the change in the Cash line on the balance sheet between opening and closing periods — always, exactly.

Link 4 — Capex flows from CFI to PP&E on the Balance Sheet

Capex (cash outflow in CFI) increases PP&E on the balance sheet. D&A then reduces PP&E each period and flows through the P&L as an expense, reducing net income.

Link 5 — Debt changes flow through CFF and the Balance Sheet simultaneously

Debt raised or repaid in CFF simultaneously changes the debt balance on the balance sheet. Interest paid reduces net income through the P&L.

The Error Check

If your balance sheet does not balance (Assets not equal to Liabilities plus Equity), one of these five links is wrong. Diagnosing balance sheet breaks quickly is one of the most important skills a junior analyst develops in their first months.

The Most-Missed Interview Link

Candidates say "net income goes to retained earnings" — correct — but forget it also flows to the CFS as the starting line. Both happen simultaneously. Missing the CFS link is the tell that separates genuine understanding from memorisation. State both every time.
